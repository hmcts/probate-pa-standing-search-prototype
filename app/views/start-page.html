Stop a grant of probate

Use this service to stop an application for a grant of probate. This is known as applying for a 'caveat'.

You may want to stop an application for a grant of probate when:

  • you think the person who made the will was being influenced by someone else or was not able to make their own decisions
  • the person applying for probate refuses to share a copy of the will with you
  • you think the person applying won’t be able to carry out the instructions in the will
  • you think the person applying is not eligible to apply if there was no will (see who inherits if there is no will)

These are the most common reasons, but there may be other reasons too.

You should always try to sort things out with the person applying for probate before you stop their application.

Warning You may have to pay legal costs if you stop an application without a good reason.

You will need:

  • the full name of the person who died (and any other names they were known by)
  • the exact date they died
  • a home address in England and Wales

Fees

It costs £20 to stop an application for a grant of probate. This will last for 6 months.

You will need to apply again if you want to stop the application for a further 6 months.

You'll need to pay online by debit or credit card.
