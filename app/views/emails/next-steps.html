What happens now

We'll stop the application for probate.

You should try and sort things out with the person applying for probate if you can. We also recommend that you get legal advice.

What may happen next

If you are unable to resolve things with the person applying for probate, they may give you a 'warning'.

You have 20 days to respond to a warning. You have 2 choices:

1. You can 'enter an appearance'. You may do this if you believe that that the person who died had different intentions to those held by the person currently applying for probate, For example, if you think the will is not valid.

2. You can 'request a summons for directions'. You may do this if you want to apply for probate instead of the person who started the application. For example if you are also named as an executor in the will of the person who died.

If you receive a warning, we will send you more information about how you can respond. However, we won't be able to give you legal advice.
